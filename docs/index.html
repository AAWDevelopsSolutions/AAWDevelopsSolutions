Summer 2013…

Emma Wood, Ventura, CA (2013)

It was a warm afternoon in the scenic setting of Montecito, California. My folks were attending a work-related party, subsequently extending a plus-one to my younger brother & me. Certainly, it would not take too long to realize that inviting a couple of rambunctious teenagers to a mansion in Montecito would be a recipe for a disaster. Ironically, fate would have it that what was a recipe for disaster in 2013, would evolve to become my formula for success in the years to follow...
After a short half-hour drive up the southern California coast, we arrived at our destination. Despite the modesty of the house’s exterior, upon entering the backyard area, I was enchanted by an unforgettable summertime spectacle. From the artificial moss cushioning the dense swimming pool sediment, to the glowing greenhouse guarded by the tyrannous Tiki’s towering over the relatively isolated stone-path, the landscape was nothing short of Shangri-La. Alas, on planet Earth, all good things must come to an end. As the sun began to set, I decided it was time to find another activity to enjoy. Venturing into the nearby opened garage as a means of seeking shelter from the rapidly cooling air, my attention was quickly diverted to the sight of a perfectly inflated soccer ball lying innocently on the garage floor.

The Soccer Academy: Your's truly traning with two players who I thought were the GOAT's (2015)

At the time, I had just begun juggling as a means of practicing my soccer skills. And while I probably did not have a personal record of anything more than twenty consecutive touches on an air-borne ball, in that moment, I thought I was Cristiano Ronaldo on #5 magazine.
After juggling the ball for a few minutes, one of my final touches on the soccer ball resulted in the ball finding its way to the sloping roof of the one-story mansion. As I watched the ball roll its way directly over the heads of the unsuspecting live-band members, I could only shield my eyes away from the painfully predictable sequence that followed. Quickly, my stint of showboating turned into a state of chagrin. Not too long after retrieving the ball, it seemed as if I was exiled from the activities of the outdoors, which obviously catered to an older audience. My fate was sealed by the party-hosts when they approached me, in a much more friendly way than I deserved, suggesting I “play with the boys.”
As I was escorted to the neutrally toned door, I couldn’t help but feel like I was being taken to the juvenile detention facility. I did not want to associate with the other boys, for if I did, I would’ve done so from the get-go. I knew virtually nothing about them, except that they competed for the rivaling Santa Barbara Junior Life-Guards—a reason I believed was substantial enough to avoid association altogether. Nonetheless, after a few knocks on the door, and a brief introduction, I found myself coerced to embrace the other side of it.

Black Ops II Zombies Reveal: Photo from official COD YouTuber trailer (2012)

The bedroom was relatively small to the house, no bigger than 100 square feet. To my spatial surprise, there were about half a dozen others around my age in this room. Akin to moths swarming around a night-light, the large radiating television bewitched my peers' curiosity. And how could it not? Playing Black Ops II Zombies during its heydays on four-way split screen was a noteworthy experience that any gamer would remember…
After enduring a few games of being a mere spectator, my newly acquainted allies begrudgingly passed me the sticks. Up until that point, my experience playing zombies was practically zero. In fact, the only Call-of-Duty game I had played was Call-of-Duty Modern Warfare, which was only single-player on the Wii console. Nevertheless, the passing of the controller felt like an obligatory duty to attempt the locals’ quest of surviving and exterminating the endless waves of mutant zombies on the infamous Black Ops II map: Tranzit.
To say I was dead weight in my first matches would be an insult to inanimate objects; I was a liability. From constantly dying at the mystery box in hopes of obtaining a rare gun that would counter-balance my obvious lack of skill, to burning all my ammunition in record-time, to frequently missing the bus transporting the team to different sections of the map, there was no doubt that if I was the last alive in the round, the game was as good as finished. Even though being at the bottom of the leaderboard every time was surely embarrassing, it became more manageable as I improved.
A few hours later, what was once perceived as a room that held me captive, was now a room that I did not want to vacate. Sooner or later, my parents convinced me to leave, to which I respectfully returned the controller, and fled the house with my family. And while this would be the last time I ever interacted with this group of kids, it would be the first time I acknowledged the unmistakable: I was destined to be an ambassador for gamers!

My Projects

If two things are unavoidable in this world, certainly they are death and taxes. While the date to pay taxes is one to be anticipated, medical problems can arise at the most unexpected and devastating times in one’s life. Time for an Upgrade is a dedicated mobile application programmed to inspire positive health outcomes through the use of non-fungible tokens (NFT’s). The hyperlink in the title shows the non-provisional patent associated with my ideas.


Both of my parents were Oncologists; and despite scratching the surface of clinical affairs as an intern at Agilent in 2018, I was never quite as passionate about Molecular Biology as my parents were. Nonetheless, from my unique interests and skills, the opportunity to pay homage to their altruistic ambitions has manifested itself. From two years of working as a dishwasher in a skilled-nursing facility, to fervently studying psychology books pertaining to game-design, it is my ultimate goal in becoming a mobile applications developer is to be able to create this mobile application!


At it’s core, the problem that Time for an Upgrade resolves is, what I like to call: “Age-induced Depreciation.” This is no revolutionary concept. While the appeal of becoming older has always carried a negative stigma, the time for a solution to be made is now! With advancements in medical technology, the prevalence of social media, and the rapid adoption of mobile devices amongst younger generations, it’s no surprise that elderly people will increase in population, live longer lives, and continue to assimilate to the nuances of the digital world.

Super Seeds

I’ve always been skinny, but I’ve never been starving. Living a lavish life in the United States, it’s easy to neglect the hundreds of millions of people who suffer from food insecurity around the world. It was for this very reason that I chose to focus my social-enterprise on a solution to address this wide-spread and prolonged problem. Given that Super Seeds is a social-enterprise, this project will most likely be pursued when I have acquired the necessary resources to apply for governmental grants and private equity loans. As of right now, becoming a mobile applications developer is my immediate priority!


Nevertheless, the extent of my research on this project was anything but topical. The ace up my sleeve for this project was undoubtedly obtaining primary research from interviewing a former native Ethiopian tribe leader, a serendipitous result of becoming connected with the neighboring Christian church. This enabled me to better understand the pro’s and con’s of the ‘subsistence-farming’ employed by the natives of Sub-Saharan Africa.


Unlike Time for an Upgrade, Super Seeds is not a technologically complex idea. The idea is very simple: acquire local partnerships to eventually build a syndicate of last-mile delivery channels that enable the accessibility of raw materials needed to support subsistence farming. From a logistical standpoint, on the other hand, it becomes a strategic nightmare; even for someone who enjoys puzzles like myself! With that said, I am optimistic that my social-skills and broad travel experiences are the invaluable intangibles necessary to forging symbiotic relationships with the locals. Moreover, with the time I have invested into identifying the problem, as well as the time I have spent enumerating a tentative financial roadmap, I believe I am adjourning Super Seeds in at an appropriate milestone to resume in the future!
