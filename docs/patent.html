BACKGROUND [0002] Embodiments of the invention described in this specification relate generally to augmented reality games, and more particularly, to an augmented reality game (ARG) and non-fungible token (NFT) badge earning motivational system and positive-feedback mechanisms with respect to the healthcare industry. [0003] Challenges can be effective in stimulating mental processes that keep people bright and active by engaging their minds and easing boredom. The Internet is rife with challenges. For instance, many video streams challenge viewers in a variety of ways. Some of these challenge-prompting streamers are very popular (e.g., Mr. Beast, BeardMeetsFood, Ryan Trahan, etc.). Clearly, the relative popularity of the challenge-producing content streamers would seem to suggest that people of all ages like challenges. [0004] Video game users (or “gamers”) implicitly understand that the games they play most are games that motivate them to achieve or explore or otherwise continue engaging in the challenges presented in the game format. This is known as a positive-feedback mechanism and, in the context of gaming, has been coined as the “gamification” effect. When gamification or other positive-feedback mechanisms are applied outside the field of video games, the results are similar in that they effectively spur intrinsic motivation in individuals faced with tasks, work, or other challenges in real life (referred to as “IRL”, meaning things that happen or occur in the physical world). In terms of the psychology of intrinsic motivation, there exists a little-known fact that games can be used in a wider context (e.g., IRL or in device-based contexts, such as augmented reality applications, virtual reality applications, etc.) as a vehicle for reinforcing positive health outcomes. [0005] However, there is a segment of the population that is not typically associated 1 with the streamers noted above or any sort of gamification platform. Thus, while the concept of WELCHER.ANTHONY-LZ.001NP gamification has proven its effectiveness for more than a decade, the use of gamification has yet to be applied to the one group of people who would benefit most from their application—that is, people seeking long-term medical treatment and care. People who require long-term medical care are often faced with despair and depression. In many cases, there are physical constraints that prevent them from living a fulfilling life. Consequently, most elderly patients in long-term medical care and assisted living suffer from lack of mental stimulation and become bored, depressed, and/or lose cognitive skills. In many respects, these patients are unchallenged, which only furthers the despair many feel. [0006] Therefore, what is needed is a way to incorporate gamification into a challenge platform for elderly patients and to reward successful completion of challenges with digital cosmetic assets for those patients who can be tasked to earn them. BRIEF DESCRIPTION [0007] Some embodiments of the invention include a novel augmented reality game (ARG) and non-fungible token (NFT) badge earning motivational system and an ARG and NFT badge earning motivational user and administrator interactive method are disclosed for instilling intrinsic motivation by completing a challenge to earn a digital badge as a cosmetic asset reward for completing the challenge. [0008] In some embodiments, the ARG and NFT badge earning motivational system comprises an ARG software application (also referred to as the “mobile ARG app”, the “desktop ARG program”, the “desktop ARG application”, and/or the “ARG program”) that utilizes earned digital cosmetic assets (otherwise referred to as “badges”, “rewards”, “points”, “NFTs”, etc.) to instill intrinsic motivation in users. The ARG software application is designed with objectives for patients who are primarily confined to elder care institutions, such as assisted living residences, or medical facilities. The ARG software application enables users to engage in step-level game challenges to achieve objectives and offers game “rewards”, thereby encouraging the user in efforts to improve both their mental and physical abilities, as well as provide intrinsic satisfaction in meeting challenges and rewarded for achievements (thereby maintaining intrinsic motivation of the users). Objectives may be defined by medical practitioners who are responsible for patients, administrators at facilities where the patients reside, and/or by engineers of the ARG software application. Objectives are typically assigned to individual patients by practitioners. In this specification, the term “practitioners” refers generally to caregivers, certified nursing 2 assistants (CNAs), licensed and/or registered nurses (RNs), medical doctors (MDs), and other WELCHER.ANTHONY-LZ.001NP medical practitioners, but is also extended to include ancillary representatives of institutions, such as administrators, program directors, etc. Regardless of which type of practitioner has assigned objectives to patients, when those patients engage with the ARG software application, they focus on completing the challenge(s) of those objectives. In some embodiments, patients need to complete each challenge to be rewarded. In some other embodiments, a variable completion percentage for a given challenge by a patient may yield a proportionate reward. [0032] As stated above, currently, people who require long-term medical care are often faced with despair and depression. In many cases, there are physical constraints that prevent them from living a fulfilling life. Consequently, most elderly patients in long-term medical care and assisted living suffer from lack of mental stimulation and become bored, depressed, and/or lose cognitive skills. In many respects, these patients are unchallenged, which only furthers the despair many feel. Embodiments of the ARG and NFT badge earning motivational system described in this specification solve such problems by providing the positive effects of gamification in augmented reality games (ARGs) to challenge long-term care patients (also referred to as “players”) with tasks that are assigned by medical practitioners (also referred to as “operators” or “administrators”) and rewarding the players for completion (or partial completion) of the assigned tasks. Specifically, players are rewarded with digital cosmetic assets for successful completion (or partial completion) of the challenges/tasks. In some embodiments, the digital cosmetic assets comprise non-fungible tokens (NFTs) with a visual icon (referred to as a “badge”) which the player can display in an interface of the ARG and on a community dashboard collectively viewable by all players and operators. [0033] The ARG and NFT badge earning motivational system differs from currently existing games and ARGs, which typically offer “one size fits all” types of rewards for challenges that are pushed out to all users and are not mapped to the individual needs and capabilities of individual users. For instance, other existing games and ARGs offer virtual rewards, but do not offer various levels of competition based on age or mental acuity. But consider the case of individual users who are typically elderly patients with physical and/or cognitive setbacks or who are otherwise declining in various age-related ways. How can “one 8 WELCHER.ANTHONY-LZ.001NP size fits all” games work for the particular needs of such elderly patients? Furthermore, the existing games and ARGs are solely focused on the user and, therefore, do not have any built-in mechanism that involves a user’s care team or resident administrators. Thus, there needs to be a way to customize or personalize the level or degree of challenge anticipated for particular tasks, such that the tasks/challenges assigned to particular patients are customized or personalized to meet the needs and desires of the particular patient, as identified by both the patient and their care team. [0034] Addressing this issue, the ARG and NFT badge earning motivational system described in this specification offers rewards, but not “one size fits all” type of rewards of the existing games and ARGs. Instead, the ARG and NFT badge earning motivational system goes far beyond the simple virtual rewards-based ARGs to provide valued rewards tailored to particular players/patients (based on age and/or mental acuity and other factors). For instance, the rewards may include customized rewards that are valued by players, such as (without limitation) virtual coins, badges, points, or advancement (e.g., advancing a level). These customized rewards are designed to provide intrinsic motivation to individual players, many of whom vary in age and/or mental capacity or even in physical health/capacity, such as may vary widely among any group of players, especially the elderly or disabled. [0035] In some embodiments, the ARG and NFT badge earning motivational system customizes the rewards offered to players based on a multitude of factors including (i) the goals of the player with respect to the player’s level/capacity (in one or more health, mobility, or mental aspects), (ii) the difficulty of the task, and potentially (iii) the discretion of the care team (operators and other medical/long term care practitioners), who can set various challenges for the patient or resident of the medical facility. At the operator’s discretion, assigned challenges can entail the patient’s cooperation in various ways, such as physical exercises (meaning, exercises to be performed by the patient in real life, or “IRL” for short, which is used to refer to things that happen in the physical world as opposed to “device-based” meaning online or in a virtual context), brain activities (IRL or device-based), board games (IRL or device-based), jigsaw puzzles (IRL or device-based), affirmations of mental fortitude (IRL or device-based), ARGs (combination of IRL and device-based), VR games (only device-based), etc. Rewards are allotted commensurate with the task at hand and the variable degree of challenge the task would present to a particular patient. For instance, there may be no reward offered for a task of walking down the hallway (IRL) to a patient who is completely ambulatory and able to move freely about on a 9 WELCHER.ANTHONY-LZ.001NP regular basis, but for another patient whose mobility is limited, the reward can be significant to suitably match the level of exertion required, given their more frail state. In this way, the ARGs (or other games or tasks) and levels of challenges are customized (or personalized) to meet the needs and desires of the patient, identified by both the patient and their care team. [0036] Also, elderly patients and others confined to long-term care are often invited to participate in group activities, such as bingo, card games, or other games of chance. While activities, such as movies, may be entertaining, they are passive and routinely fail to stimulate the minds of viewers. Nevertheless, there is an underappreciated aspect about social activities regardless of how much immersion they provide. In some embodiments, the ARG and NFT badge earning motivational system of the present disclosure provides rewards for group activities, as well as mental challenges through the ARG software application and/or community dashboard (or other accessible patient/client-side software application, including web or other) at various levels, and promotes social interactions among the participants, rewarding the user with points or badges upon successful completion. In addition, the patient’s care team will have more visibility into the needs and abilities of their patients and residents, thereby allowing them to customize the tasks assigned to individual patients/residents which provide challenges at a level suitable for the capacity of the individual patient/resident, along with enabling tracking of their progress. [0044] As noted above, Figure 3 conceptually illustrates an exemplary NFT skill badge, specifically, the “Triple Down” NFT badge 300. The “Triple Down” NFT badge 300 provides an example of an obtainable NFT badge, and how it may appear in the community dashboard or in the ARG software application. Note, however, the NFT badges of the ARG and NFT badge earning motivational system are unlike conventional NFTs, in that these badges are not available through purchase, trading, or any type of other mercenary transaction. There is a single authorizing entity, which in this case is a server of the ARG and NFT badge earning motivational system and all transactions are written to the blockchain without proof of work from one of several nodes. Also, the NFT badge is unique, quite similar to conventional NFTs. Yet, the restrictions for trading, purchases, or otherwise transacting in NFTs are intended to ensure that users earn their NFT badges as a reward for completion of challenges. In this way, the accumulation of NFT badges can serve to spur the intrinsic motivation of users to complete the challenges they are assigned, as a key objective of the embodiments described herein. While the exemplary NFT badge shown in this figure is referred to as the “Triple Down” NFT badge 300, in this particular example, the naming convention is not as important as the underlying motivation to complete the challenge by the user. Thus, “Triple Down” is merely a play on words from arena-battle video games. Nevertheless, in actual practice, it is possible for a user to earn 13 WELCHER.ANTHONY-LZ.001NP the “Triple Down” NFT badge 300 by, for example, completing a challenge to eat three meals a day for seven days straight (a full week). Naturally, this challenge may be completely unneeded for a patient who routinely eats three meals per day. Thus, each patient user is assigned challenges that are customized to their condition and abilities. Accordingly, there exist many different challenges designed for all kinds of people. Furthermore, administrators can design new custom challenges as needed, registering new NFT badges for recording on the blockchain.
